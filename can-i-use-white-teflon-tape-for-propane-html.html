Can I use white Teflon tape for propane?

 Posted on June 21, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1125 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Answer

It is not recommended to use plumber’s Teflon tape on gas pipe fittings because the tape will deteriorate with time and allow gas vapours to escape through the fitting. It is white in colour and thinner than the yellow gas-rated Teflon tape that is used by plumbers. Gas leaks from pipelines and connections are very hazardous and may result in an explosion.

PTFE (Teflon) tape/pipe dope is only required on pipe thread fittings and is not required on any other fittings. The use of tape on the flare fitting may not result in any issues, but I would not take the risk, particularly when dealing with gas.
